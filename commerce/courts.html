Local Courts
  • No Jury

Examples

  • Minor offenses (e.g., traffic violations, petty theft).
  • Preliminary hearings for more serious crimes.
  • Committal proceedings (determining if there's enough evidence for a trial in a higher court).
  • Small claims disputes (e.g., debt collection, property damage).
  • Some landlord-tenant disputes.
  • Certain types of family law matters.
  • Issuing warrants.
  • Handling some administrative matters.

Courts within Local Courts

Children's Court

Court focused on children's well-being in legal cases.

  • Child's best interest.
  • Family support.
  • Social services.
  • Rehabilitation.

Drug Court

Rehab for non-violent drug offenses.

  • Treatment, not just jail.
  • Supervised recovery.
  • Regular court checks and drug tests.
  • Support programs.
  • Focus on change.
  • Team effort.
  • Reduces repeat offenses.

Corners Court

This court helps solve small crimes and neighbor problems by working with the community.

  • Focuses on fixing problems, not just punishing.
  • Uses talks to help people agree.
  • Has regular meetings with the community.
  • Helps people get help for their problems.
  • Makes people responsible by having them do community service or pay back.
  • Brings together people from the community, helpers, and lawyers.
  • Tries to make the neighborhood better and stop problems from happening again.
  • Deals with small crimes and neighbor problems.
