Court Hierarchy

    Lowest To Highest

  • Local Court

  • District Court
  • Supreme Court
  • High Court

Local Courts

  • Has a Magistrate instead of judge.
  • No jury.
  • Civil Cases up to $100k.
  • Deals with "summary offenses."
  • Does not hear appeals (because it's the lowest court).

Examples

  • Traffic offenses.
  • Minor assaults.
  • Disorderly conduct.
  • Shoplifting (of low-value items).
  • Trespassing.
  • Minor drug possession.

District Court

  • Hears appeals from the local court.
  • Deals with cases from $100k to $750k.
  • Jury is randomly selected from citizens.

Supreme Court

  • Hears appeals from the district court.
  • Deals with cases over $750k+.
  • Jury is randomly selected from citizens.

High Court

  • Hears appeals from the supreme court.
  • Deals with cases over $750k+.
  • No jury.
  • The federal court of australia that applies to the entire country.
  • Can create common law.
  • Can change the constitution.
  • Has multiple judges (6 judges and 1 chief justice).

Specialized Courts

Children's Court

Court focused on children's well-being in legal cases.

  • Deals with 18 and under (minors) with their legal matters.
  • Focus on rehabilitation.
  • Is closed to the media and public.

Drug Court

Rehab for non-violent drug offenses.

  • Deals with non-violent drug offenses.
  • Focus on rehabilitation.

Corners Court

This court helps solve small crimes and neighbor problems by working with the community.

  • Deals with inspecting dead bodies which died from mysterious causes.

Family Court

  • Deals with divorce.
  • Deals with child custody.
