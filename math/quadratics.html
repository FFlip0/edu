quadratics

The core of quadratics in having more than one solution for a single variable. There are three main ways to solve quadratics; Factorization, quadratic equation and by completing the square (I haven't done the "completing the square" method yet).

Factorization

Factorization is a process where you take different things in common. The end goal is so that multiple terms (even equations in brackets) multiply each other to equal zero.

7.1

$$\sqrt{-1} = i$$ (imaginary numbers)

1)

$$x^2=36$$ $$x=\sqrt{36}$$ $$x=6,-6,=\pm6$$

2)

$$x^2-81=0$$ $$x^2=81$$ $$x=\sqrt{81}$$ $$x=\pm9$$

3)

$$4x^2-49=0$$ $$4x^2=49$$ $$x^2=\frac{49}{4}$$ $$x=\sqrt{\frac{49}{4}}$$ $$x=\frac{\sqrt{49}}{\sqrt{4}}$$ $$x=\pm\frac{7}{2}$$

4)

$$x^2+4=0$$ $$x^2=-4$$ $$x=\sqrt{-4}$$ (no two numbers multiply to give -4, this is called an imaginary number) $$x=\sqrt{4}\sqrt{-1}$$ $$x=\pm2\sqrt{-1}$$ $$x=\pm2i$$

5)

$$(x-5)^2=9$$ $$x-5=\sqrt{9}$$ $$x-5=\pm3$$ $$x-5=3$$ $$x=8$$

$$(x-5)^2=9$$ $$x-5=\sqrt{9}$$ $$x-5=\pm3$$ $$x-5=-3$$ $$x=2$$

6)

$$(y+\frac{3}{4})^2=\frac{25}{16}$$ $$y+\frac{3}{4}=\sqrt{\frac{25}{16}}=\frac{\sqrt{25}}{\sqrt{16}}=\pm\frac{5}{4}$$

$$(y+\frac{3}{4})^2=\frac{25}{16}$$ $$y+\frac{3}{4}=\sqrt{\frac{25}{16}}=\frac{\sqrt{25}}{\sqrt{16}}=\pm\frac{5}{4}$$
